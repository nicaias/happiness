Taking Action


How to disrupt the habit of rumination using the WOOP method and self-compassion

The WOOP method, developed by Gabriele Oettingen, is an acronym used to help you achieve your goals by using intention implementation and mental contrasting strategies. You are able to directly identify both your challenges and a plan of action when they occur in your journey. Most often, these plans follow an "if-then" format, where each obstacle is met with a specific solution.

What is the desired outcome from achieveing your wish? What feelings will you experience? How will your future self be when they finish the WOOP plan?

List at least 3 specific obstacles that you know will come up while you are trying to achieve your goal. This is called mental constrasting, where you compare your goal to the obstacles you expect to experience along the way.

Using an "if-then" format, create specific plans to solve each one of your listed obstacles. In this way, you will know what to do when you encounter your obstacles.

Self-Compassion


What is self-compassion and how we can use it to shape our belief systems?


The Definition

Self-compassion consists of being supportive of oneself during feelings of pain and suffering. Instead of treating decisions with self-judgement, it approaches situations with emotional availability and support, as if we are acting compassionately toward someone we love.


What does this mean?

Self-compassion allows us to see the humanity within ourselves. Many times, we think we are alone in our battles, but this is not the case. Through self-compassion, we must remember that everyone struggles and it is a core part of being human. It reminds us to accept ourselves and give ourselves grace, especially during times of hardship.
